We are MA Bandit,

a probability startup to generate risk metrics

MA Bandit, the multi armed bandit problem, is at the root of everything we do. We seek the optimal balance of risk and reward, taking away assumptions of regression to get to the truth of market psychology.

Tweets and Posts

The internet is scraped for any post relating to a stock. Other indicators are conditioned on similar word frequencies, relative placement, and volume deviatons. This let’s our indicators act as more effective estimators read more 
Hurst Cycles Movement pivots are predicted with cycles. Just as cycles are found in stock movements similar cycles are found in word choice in the blogosphere. We take advantage of this to identify cycles proabilistically. read more 
Moving Averages After market pivots are identified, moving averages used to give further evidence for supply and demand imbalances. High kurtosis bids are also compared to previous movements with similar statistics. read more 
It’s anything but sentiment analysis But it does use tweets, and technical indicators, and some basic probability. As Archimedes says: ‘Give me a neural network large enough and some data on which to train it and I will predict the earth’
The truth is our methods are not at all complex. Reliable prediction just needs predictive data. We find the indicators that work and put our energy into making sure they are recorded correctly. Through simple baysean summation, bad data doesn’t hurt our predictive capabilities and the good data, consistently recorded, produce results that correlate strongly with movements for the next day.
