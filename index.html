About MA Bandit


Some have said that the market is impossible to predict: that no matter what tools you develop you can’t beat the curve. It is an attitude that has come about after a long history of failure from the plethora of guide to the stock market books that have come out and definitely not changed the world. There is no silver bullet to trading, but if you treat the stock market like an art form, all you are doing is gambling. We don’t gamble.


Have you ever heard of the standard model for particle physics? The concept is cool; it is an incredibly accurate coupling equation that can tell you all about the energy transfer of particles. This is an incredibly impressive feat of science, and it gives a lot of hope for systematizing complex movement—just like the movement we see in the stock market.

Now take a step back and ask yourself what moves market prices. Tax policies? War? Crop yields in Guam? Yes, but only indirectly. It’s people who move the market; those events only influence people. What we are developing now are barometers of the people: crucial elements of our standard model.

We took everyone who trades and split them up into 3 groups that we can clearly differentiate: employees, institutions (such as hedge funds), and “boutique” traders. From there we analyzed which forces act on each group. For example, many people overlook the fact that, since career traders put money in the market, when they read things like technical indicators or news reports and then act on that information, they may actually be creating a self fulfilling prophecy!

Now we are but a humble group of math and physics nerds wondering how many things you can model in this fashion. Markets are great because they provide fantastic feedback, even more than historical tests, because if you see how much money we made you can see exactly how well our trust was placed. We hope to expand all fields by introducing rigor where rigor was once considered impossible.

Projects


VISION: we can look at real world events that occur at recurring times and see how they have coupled to a group. Social security has to keep a certain amount of cash at the time which it pays back its recipients, and this causes a noticeable and recurring drop in a stock price if you know which stocks said fund is investing in. We are developing programs which will predict future stock values based on the sentiments of one of the main groups which influences stock prices: the general public. Through data gathering and statistical analysis, we believe we can make predictions that are accurate enough to consistently beat the market. Something that says what we do but not super technical but also not super vague. An important aspect of our methodology is that we gather our data from predictors who are not in a testing environment. Test subjects who are aware that they are being observed act differently than those who are not, so by gathering our data from sites where people post predictions voluntarily, we are able to avoid any unwanted influence we may have on predictions. Additionally, with this approach we do not introduce any incentives for predictors which may lead to data being less useful.

RESULTS: Our main project will gather data from public stock prediction websites in order to develop an extensive database of past predictions and their accuracy. From these data, we can cross examine each individual predictor’s past accuracy against their current predictions in order to develop an array of possible prices a stock will hit in any given time period. This way, instead of giving a single prediction, the investor can see the percent chances of a variety of prices for the stock they are interested in, and thus make a far more informed decision. When our program is fully operational, we will evaluate it by back testing our algorithm against past events in the stock market. Using this method, we can see how accurate our percentages are to the actual movements of stocks, and post those results here.
